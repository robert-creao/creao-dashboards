The Story

A coach who became a guide.

For thirty years, Kevin Skeens has been in the gym before the lights come on and after the last whistle blows. What started as volleyball coaching turned into something deeper — a methodology for performing under pressure that draws from sports psychology, lived experience, and the quiet work of becoming the person you want to be.

Kevin's framework was built rep by rep, with collegiate athletes, club teams, and elite competitors. It was sharpened through certification with the International Mental Game Coaching Association, and it found its heart at Casa Hogar — an orphanage in Manzanillo, Mexico, where he learned what really matters when the scoreboard goes dark.

Today he brings that work to stages, teams, and organizations who want more than a pep talk. They want a system. They want a shift. They want to walk in different than they walked out.

— Kevin Skeens, founder of Advance Sport IQ

The Methodology

Built for the moment.

A three-step system for performing when it matters — built on the truth that great performances aren't created on game day. They're built long before the lights come on.

01

Practice with Intention

Train the skill. Train the focus.

Most athletes go through practice. Elite athletes attack practice with purpose. Specific goals plus reflection sharpen attention — and focused attention accelerates everything that comes after.

  • Practice Intention Goals (P.I.G.)
  • Reflection as a daily skill
  • Wiring the brain for game day
02

Build Away from the Court

Confidence is built in private.

What an athlete does outside of practice is what separates average from elite. Game confidence doesn't magically appear — it's manufactured daily, through deliberate work on mind, body, and identity.

  • Visualization & mental rehearsal
  • Breathwork & emotional regulation
  • Journaling the inner game
03

Shift on Game Day

Trust. Don't think.

Game day is not for analyzing. It's for trusting. The shift from front brain to back brain — from technical to instinctive — is the difference between athletes who freeze and athletes who fly.

  • Front brain to back brain shift
  • Cue words and the inner coach
  • Resetting after mistakes

Built for the Moment sits at the center of the Shatter Expectations methodology — surrounded by the frameworks Kevin has refined over thirty years: OWN IT (the fire within), The Inner Coach, The 5 Things I Can Control, LIT (Learning · Invest · Take Risks), and The Power Connection.

"Emotional reactions that are not kept in check are the bullet holes that drain the tank of confidence."

— Coach Kevin Skeens
